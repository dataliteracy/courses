Data literacy to enhance data-driven decision-making

Nepal Data Literacy Program is designed to catalyze stronger data-driven decision-making by government and non-government actors (mass media, civil society, and academia) through targeted Data Literacy and data systems strengthening. The program comprises a modular, customizable pedagogy to support both technical skills-building and efforts to inaugurate a ‘culture of data use’ among targeted stakeholders.

Data, more specifically, government data can play an instrumental role in helping Nepal government advance its development objectives and its aim of achieving double-digit growth rate, foster partnership among various government agencies, and enable and environment for robust tracking and monitoring of progress.


Supported by The World Bank

Data literacy can support Nepal government’s priorities by fostering an environment where citizens have the knowledge to use data for evidence-based decision making and to increase transparency and accountability in public.
